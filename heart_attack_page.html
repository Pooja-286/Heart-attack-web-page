What is a Heart Attack?

A heart attack (myocardial infarction) happens when blood flow to part of the heart is blocked ‚Äî often by a blood clot ‚Äî causing damage to the heart muscle. Prompt recognition and action save lives.

Quick facts

  • Also called: myocardial infarction (MI)
  • Typical cause: blocked coronary artery
  • Treatment window: earlier treatment = better outcomes
  • Common tests: ECG, blood troponin, angiography
  • Treatments: aspirin, clot-busting drugs, stents

Symptoms ‚Äî Know the warning signs

Symptoms can vary. Classic and less-typical signs include:

  • Chest discomfort ‚Äî pressure, squeezing, fullness or pain
  • Pain spreading to shoulders, neck, jaw, or arms
  • Shortness of breath
  • Cold sweat, nausea or lightheadedness
  • Unusual fatigue (especially in women)
  • Sudden anxiety or a feeling of impending doom

Important: If you suspect a heart attack ‚Äî call emergency services immediately. While waiting, chew an aspirin (if not allergic) and try to stay calm.

What to do in an emergency

  1. Call your local emergency number immediately.
  2. Ask someone to get you medical help. If you're alone, chew an aspirin unless contraindicated.
  3. If you lose consciousness and someone nearby is trained, begin CPR.

Preventive precautions

  • Quit smoking ‚Äî decreases risk significantly.
  • Stay active ‚Äî aim for 150 mins of moderate exercise weekly.
  • Eat heart-healthy ‚Äî more vegetables, whole grains, lean proteins; less salt & processed foods.
  • Manage blood pressure, cholesterol & diabetes ‚Äî regular checkups and medication adherence.
  • Maintain healthy weight and manage stress with sleep, mindfulness, or counselling.

When to see your doctor

Periodic checkups are important if you have risk factors: high blood pressure, high cholesterol, family history of heart disease, diabetes, or if you're a smoker. Discuss tests and an individualised plan.
